GOALS

Tell the story of cities through their architecture

Faded Cities chooses cities whose rich and varied history have been overshadowed by their contemporary issues to explore and showcase. We believe their stories are worth telling. Our explorations involve a mix of technical and interactive media that best showcases the city’s history and people.

Document the city’s architecture and history

The featured cities often do not have comprehensive or easily accessible documentation. We aim to catalogue and document the histories, architectural styles and locations of each city’s most notable buildings. Our work takes the form of architectural research, on-site investigation and collaboration with local architects, historians and creatives.

Learn from history

Understanding the multi-faceted history of each city can help us better understand its present and ultimately provide insight into its future. We hope our specific insights and research on each city’s urban development and landscape will shed light on the contemporary issues it faces and point in the direction of a solution.

Provide a framework for sustainable urban regeneration and preservation

Faded Cities believes in historical and architectural preservation not only to shed light on the past but also to create a framework for more livable and sustainable cities in the present and future. Our architectural and urban research gives us insight and allows us to provide suggestions for more sustainable cities that avoid the development errors of the past and present.

Create a community of practitioners

By conducting thorough research into the architecture of cities we build a comprehensive network of architects, historians and creatives who are similarly committed to the past, present and future urban landscape of their cities.
